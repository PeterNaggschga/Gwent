144 <string name="rules_general_text"><![CDATA[<p>Gwent is an old card game especially fancied by dwarfs. It represents the confrontation of two opposing armies. The players are generals and cards represent their units. Every player uses his own unique deck of cards. Decks can be created for each of the four factions: Monsters, Nilfgaardian Empire, Northern Kingdoms and Scoia\'tael. Each faction has unique cards which lead to different play styles. Moreover, there are cards that can be used for every faction. Your goal is to win two rounds out of three by having more strength than your opponent.</p>]]></string>
145 <string name="rules_course_text"><![CDATA[<p>At the beginning of the first round each player draws ten cards from their deck. After drawing, you can discard two cards and draw a new card for each. This step is optional, discarded cards are put back into the deck. The beginning player is determined by flipping a coin.<br/>The players place cards alternately. You have to play one card every round! If a player doesn\'t want to play a card, they have to pass and aren\'t allowed to play another card until the round has ended. When one player passes, their opponent can still play cards until they pass too. If one of the opponents uses up all of his cards, he has to pass.<br />A round ends when both players pass. The player with the highest total score wins the round. When both opponents have the same number of points, the round is seen as a win for both sides. All cards are moved to the discard pile at the end of each round. Your hand is reused in the next round—you don\'t draw new cards. The winner of the last round gets the first turn. Goal of the game is to win two rounds.</p>]]></string>

174 <string name="rules_cards_text"><![CDATA[<p>
175 There are three major types of cards: Leaders, units and Special Cards.
176 Each deck has one leader card.
177 It has a certain ability which can be activated once per game.<br />
178 Moreover, there are at least 22 Unit Cards including Heroes.
179 Heroes are immune to any boost or weakening.
180 Units are played in turns.
181 Each player has got three combat rows, one for Close combat, Ranged combat and Siege units each.
182 It can be seen on the cards which row must be used.<br />
183 Also there are Special Cards (maximum 10), \"Decoy\",
184 \"Scorch\", \"Commander\'s Horn\" and weather cards.
185 Weather cards are placed in a separate section of the board
186 because they influence both players equally.
187 Commander\'s Horn is placed at the designated combat row.</p>
188 ]]></string>
189 <string name="rules_card_abilities_text"><![CDATA[
190 <p>Some unit cards have got special abilities which are explained in the following.</p>
191 <p>Spy: <br />Must be placed on your opponent\'s battlefield (and counts toward your opponent\'s total score), and allows you to draw 2 cards extra from your deck.</p>
192 <p>Tight Bond: <br />When placed next to a card with the same name, double the strength of both cards.</p>
193 <p>Muster: <br />When played, automatically find any cards with the same name in your deck and play them instantly.</p>
194 <p>Morale Boost: <br />When played, add +1 strength to all other units in the row it is played in.</p>
195 <p>Medic: <br />Allow you to choose one card from your discard pile and play it instantly (no Heroes or Special Cards). Agile units have to be placed in the combat row they have been in before.</p>
196 <p>Commander\'s Horn: <br />Double the strength of all other units in this combat row.</p>
197 <p>Agile: <br />Can be placed in either the Close combat or the Ranged combat row. They cannot be moved once placed.</p>
198 <p>Scorch: <br />Kill the strongest card(s) in the specified row of the enemy.</p>
199 <p>Summon Avenger: <br />Summon a powerful new unit card to take their place when they are removed from the battlefield.</p>
